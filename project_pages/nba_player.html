NBA Player Status Predictor

Research project to discover which metrics are most predictive of a player's status as either "elite" or "average" in the NBA

R RMD Exploratory Data Analysis Logistic Regression

Overview

I built a logistic regression model that determines the status of a player as either "elite" or "average". I performed preliminary exploratory data analysis to determine which metrics were most likely to influence the performance of a player. The player's status is determined by their statistcal profile from the 2023-2024 season.

Problem

This project was designed to see if a player's status as either "elite" or "average" could be predicted based on their statistical profile.

Methodology

  • Upload and clean the data to remove any missing values and add the labels for each player as either "elite" or "average".
  • Visually explore the data through various plots to see how the metrics are distributed and relate to each other.
  • Train the logistic regression model on the training data and evaluate its performance on the test data.

Results

Model Specificity

Future Improvements

Due to the class imbalances between "elite" and "average" players, the model is not as effective at predicting "elite" players. Future improvements could include using a different model that is better suited for imbalanced data, such as a random forest or gradient boosting model.

To expand upon the ideas of this project, it also opens up the door to explore which historic metrics are most predictive of a player's status in future seasons. Depending on the performance of the model, it could even become useful in selecting which players to draft for fantasy leagues based on their statistical profile.
